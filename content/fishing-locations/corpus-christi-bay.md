Located around the east southern shores of Texas, the Corpus Christi Bay is 25 miles long and 3-10 miles wide. Mustang Island is to the east and Corpus Christi city is to the west of the bay. As with most bays, it is mixed with salt and freshwater. Corpus Christi is a part of a bay system called the Corpus Christi Bay system, and there are four bays: Corpus Christi Bay, Nueces Bay, Oso Bay, and Redfish Bay. Grass flats and Mud flats are common around the shores of these bays. The Corpus Christi Bay is the deepest bay in the system, with an average depth of 11 feet. 

>Christi Bay is considered by many people to be one of the windiest bays on the Texas Coast.

## Fishing Opportunities 

- Spotted speckled [[trout]] is common.
- Redfish or red drum is a very popular fish in the bay.
- Catfish is not the most common fish due the bay's close proximity to the salt water from the gulf, but they can be found sometimes.
