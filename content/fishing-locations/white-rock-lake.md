Located in east Dallas, Texas. White Rock Lake was formed by damming the White Rock Creek, and the creek was widen to form what is seen today. The lakes maximum depth is around 20 feet. There is a lot of submerged vegetation in the lake for fish to congregate and eat around. Boats are allowed and there is a 9.9 horsepower limit. For those who want to rent a kayak or paddle board, the White Rock Paddle Company has many to rent. Similar to a good number of cities in Texas, swimming is not allowed in the White Rock Lake. There are 8 fishing piers, and the park around the lake has as a variety of trails that provide access to shore fishing. 

## Fishing Opportunities 

- Largemouth Bass and Catfish are very popular species to catch in the lake, especially around the water willow and bulrush stand plants that grow out the water.
- [[crappie|Crappie]] is common in this lake, and spring is known to be a good time to catch them.
