Lady Bird Lake is on the Colorado River and it runs through downtown Austin. It has a maximum depth of around 20 feet. This freshwater lake is surrounded by both trees and skyscrapers. There's a jogging trail that runs along most of the lake's shore that provides good shore access for fisherman trying to fish from the shore. It is a very popular kayak and paddle board spot and electric motor boats with more than 5 horsepower are not allowed. Trees hanging over the edges and bridges are common hiding spots for fish in this area.

## Fishing Opportunities 

- Largemouth bass are very popular here and some say the summertime is the best time to fish for them due to them crowding around shaded areas like hover hanging trees to stay cool.
- Catfish aren't that common here but a catch is potentially possible.
- Bluegills are very common here and most beginner anglers fishing from the shore won't have a hard time catching them.
