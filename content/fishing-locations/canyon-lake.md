Canyon Lake is north of San Antonio and is located on the Guadalupe River. There are seven major parks around the shores of Canyon Lake that are maintained by the Army Corps of Engineers, and the others are managed by Comal County. It has plenty of opportunities for freshwater shoreline fishing, and an entrance or boat fee is charged at most parks around the lake/reservoir.

> Rocky ledges and shores are common around Canyon Lake.

![[assets/canyon-lake.jpg|399]]

## Fishing Opportunities 

- [[largemouth-bass|Largemouth Bass]] is the most the abundant sportfish in the reservoir.
- Blue, channel, and flathead catfish are present.
- Sunfish, such as bluegill, are present along the shallow areas.
- Rainbow trout is mainly present during the stocking season which occurs in the winters; however, some have been recorded to survive year-round near the dams in the reservoir.
