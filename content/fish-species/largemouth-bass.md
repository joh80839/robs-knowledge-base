Largemouth Bass are typically green with black blotches that form a horizontal stripe on both sides of the fish. Their bellies range in color from light green to white-ish. They have a divided dorsal fin (top fin). The front dorsal fin has nine spines and the back dorsal fin has 12 to 13 rays. They can grow up to 12 inches within their first two years. Their jaws are known to extend way beyond their eyes when open, giving a bucket mouth appearance. 

## Habitat

Largemouth like to seek protective cover under logs, underwater vegetation, and man-made structures. They enjoy calm waters, but they are resilient and can live in a variety of habitats.

### Baits for Largemouth Bass

- Artificial bait is incredibly effective, such as  big plastic worms and jigs; jigs mimic a natural fish's movement.
- For live bait, they like whole shad, minnows, and [[bluegill|blue gill]].
