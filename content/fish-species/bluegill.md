Bluegills are freshwater fish with flat-like round bodies. Their faces are dark blue-ish and they have black spots on each side of their heads. Their average life span in the wild can range from 5-8 years.  They are typically less than a foot long in nature but can possibly grow longer in ideal man-made conditions. 

## Habitat

They can be found throughout the continental United States and northern Mexico. Similar to [[crappie|crappie]], bluegill are popular in rivers, slow-moving creeks, lakes, and many freshwater areas with abundant vegetation in Texas. Shallow waters with lots of aquatic vegetation provide a safe habitat for them to hide from predators.

### Baits for Bluegill

- Bluegill are fond of worms, whether its red wigglers, night crawlers, or earthworms.
- Mealworms are a nice option, but try to use them before they turn into beetles; it's easier to hook a live mealworm than a live beetle. 

> Bluegill are most active during early mornings and late nights.
