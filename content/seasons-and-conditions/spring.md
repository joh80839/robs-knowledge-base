Many anglers consider spring a prime time to fish in Texas. As the waters get warmer, [[trout|spotted speckled trout]] around the Texas coast become more active.

For freshwater opportunities, largemouth bass, crappie, and sunfish such as bluegill are very active. Largemouth bass are likely to spend more time hunting near the shores compared to the winter months. Spring is often the last season to catch rainbow trout in most areas in Texas. Every winter, rainbow trout is dropped into a variety of freshwater areas in Texas by Texas Parks and Wildlife, and by the summer most of the trout are deceased due to the extreme heat.
